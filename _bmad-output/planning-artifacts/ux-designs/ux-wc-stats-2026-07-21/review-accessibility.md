# Accessibility Review — wc-stats

Adversarial WCAG 2.1 AA review of `DESIGN.md` + `EXPERIENCE.md` (spine pair, 2026-07-21). All contrast ratios below are computed from the declared hex tokens (WCAG relative-luminance formula), not taken from the spines' own assertions.

## Overall verdict

The spines have an unusually strong a11y skeleton — mandatory data-table alternatives, shape-dual-encoded shot outcomes, roving tabindex, `aria-sort`/`aria-expanded`, reduced-motion, 44px hit areas — but two computed hard-fails land on core paths: the heatmap's lowest step is invisible-adjacent at 1.94:1 and the ramp is *not* monotonic in lightness despite the spec's explicit claim, and the light-theme focus ring fails at 2.28:1 on the theme-invariant pitch, blinding keyboard focus on every pitch viz in light mode. Add a systemic 1.4.1 gap (leading-value and team-A/B encodings by hue alone) and a keyboard-inoperable momentum scrub that contradicts the spec's own drag ban, and this fails an AA audit as written. All are token- or sentence-level fixes; the architecture is sound.

## 1. Contrast (computed)

Verified passing (claims that hold): all ink-primary/ink-secondary text pairs both themes (7.03–17.67); accent-lime/cyan on dark canvases and pitch (7.26–14.83); all four shot-outcome markers on pitch (5.75–9.56 ≥3:1); edge-weight-1 on pitch 3.83 (claim "lowest stop ≥3:1" holds); pitch-line 3.62 (claim ≈3.6 accurate); light accents on light canvas 4.65–7.08; momentum 60%-opacity team fills over surface-raised composite to 5.62 / 4.48 vs the card (≥3:1).

- **[critical]** Heatmap step `heat-1` #2E6B50 on `pitch-surface` #0B3D2E computes to **1.94:1** — fails WCAG 1.4.11 non-text 3:1 for the lowest visible intensity band of a core Tactical viz (DESIGN → Data-visualization palette → Heatmap ramp; EXPERIENCE → Visualization Layering). Low-intensity zones will be imperceptible to low-vision users, silently converting "low activity" into "no data." *Fix:* lighten heat-1 to ≥3:1 vs #0B3D2E (e.g. ≈#3E7F60 territory) or raise the transparent-below threshold so the first rendered step is heat-2 (3.11:1) — and state the verified ratio.
- **[critical]** Light-theme focus ring on pitch markers: `--ring` maps to `accent-cyan-light` #0E7490, which computes to **2.28:1** against the theme-invariant `pitch-surface` #0B3D2E — fails 1.4.11/2.4.7 for every focusable d3 marker, node, and cluster in light mode (DESIGN → shadcn CSS-variable mapping + "The pitch is sacred ground"; EXPERIENCE → State Patterns → Focus). DESIGN's claim "lime and cyan are also ≥3:1 … against both canvases and the pitch" is true only for the *dark* cyan; the light variant fails on the pitch. *Fix:* one sentence: focus indicators rendered *on the pitch surface* always use dark-canonical `accent-cyan` #3DDBE8 (7.26:1 vs pitch) regardless of theme — consistent with the pitch being theme-invariant.
- **[high]** The heatmap ramp is **not** "monotonic in lightness" as claimed: computed relative luminance runs 0.117 → 0.218 → 0.462 → 0.788 → **0.532** — `heat-5` #F5B63C (amber) is *darker* than `heat-4` #E4EF52 (DESIGN → Heatmap ramp). Under achromatopsia/grayscale or deuteranopia, peak-intensity zones read as *less* intense than heat-4 zones, inverting the story at the most important spot; this also breaks the 1.4.1 redundancy the spec relies on. *Fix:* make heat-5 the lightest stop (e.g. drop the amber endpoint, end at a near-white yellow) or re-sequence so lightness strictly increases; re-verify computed luminance ordering.
- **[medium]** Result-chip letter color is unspecified, and the obvious dark-theme choice fails: `ink-primary` #F2F5F7 on chip fills computes to 1.62 (win), 2.72 (draw), 2.60 (loss) — all hard 4.5:1 text fails (DESIGN → Result chips; the section specifies fills and the mandatory letter but no text token). `surface-base` #0E1114 as letter color passes all three (10.68 / 6.35 / 6.66); light theme with white text passes (5.36–5.61). *Fix:* specify dark-theme chip text = `surface-base` (or `accent-lime-ink`-style near-black), light-theme = `#FFFFFF`, with computed ratios.
- **[medium]** `ink-muted` #6B757F "tertiary" text computes to 4.04 on base, 3.69 on raised, **3.30 on overlay** — below 4.5:1 everywhere except large text (DESIGN → Canvas & ink). "Tertiary" is not "disabled": if any real content (captions, meta, timestamps) ships in ink-muted at body sizes it is an AA text fail; only truly disabled controls are exempt. *Fix:* restrict ink-muted to disabled states and ≥3:1 non-text glyphs explicitly; move tertiary *text* to ink-secondary.
- **[medium]** Momentum midline: `viz-neutral` #8C979F over the composited team-A 60% fill (#7E9E30) computes to **1.03:1** — the midline (the zero-reference that gives the areas meaning) vanishes inside team A's area (DESIGN → Momentum Timeline spec). *Fix:* draw the midline in `ink-primary` (or add a 1px ink-primary casing), or render areas only up to a gap around the axis.
- **[low]** Asserted ratios overstate: ink-secondary-light on surface-overlay-light computes 6.65, not the claimed "≥7:1" (passes AA regardless); ink-primary on surface-overlay is 14.13, not "≥15:1" (DESIGN → Canvas & ink). *Fix:* restate claims as computed, or scope them to surface-base only.
- **[low]** Pitch panel vs dark canvas is 1.55:1 with "no shadow, no border" (DESIGN → Elevation & Depth: "the green field against charcoal … is contrast enough" — computed, it isn't much). Decorative boundary, not an AA fail, but low-vision users lose the panel edge. *Fix:* permit the hairline border on pitch panels in dark theme.

## 2. Color-only encoding (1.4.1)

Verified good: shot outcomes carry mandatory shape dual-encoding; edge weights dual-encode color+width; result chips carry letters; sort direction has a glyph; glossary triggers have a dotted underline.

- **[high]** "Leading side's value takes the team accent; trailing side stays ink-primary" (DESIGN → Components → Stat tile) and "leading values carry each side's entity accent" (EXPERIENCE → UJ-3 step 4, Comparison column) encode *who leads* by color alone — a direct 1.4.1 fail on the Hero head-to-head tiles (core 15-second path) and Comparison Mode. *Fix:* add a non-color leader cue (▲ glyph, weight bump, or "líder" affix in the accessible name) to every accented leading value.
- **[high]** Team A vs Team B is hue-only: viz-team-a vs viz-team-b computes 1.32:1 (dark) and **1.07:1** (light variants) — near-identical lightness, so the pair is distinguishable *only* by hue (DESIGN → Two-team contrast pair). Momentum's above/below-midline position is a redundant channel, but the recharts "Phases-of-Play comparison," pressing, and Defensive Blocks "comparative distribution charts, team accents" (EXPERIENCE → Visualization Layering) specify no direct labels, pattern, or ordering rule — legend-by-hue alone fails 1.4.1 for deuteranopic users (lime-green vs teal is risky) and grayscale. *Fix:* mandate direct series labels (team name/code at bar or line end) on every two-team recharts view, and/or a pattern/dash differential for team B.
- **[high]** One color, two meanings inside one chart: shot-goal, accent-lime, and viz-team-a are the *same hex* #C3F53C, and the Momentum Timeline specifies team-A areas in viz-team-a *and* goal markers (both teams') in shot-goal on the same surface (DESIGN → Momentum Timeline) — violating the palette's own normative rule "one color means one thing per visualization." Team B's goals render in team A's color. *Fix:* give momentum goal markers a neutral treatment (ink-primary ring/glyph) or per-team marker color matching the scoring side, and note the token aliasing hazard.
- **[medium]** Pass-network node focus "highlights that player's edges, dims the rest" (EXPERIENCE → Component Patterns → Pitch panel) — if "dims" is opacity/color only, the selected-vs-dimmed state is color/contrast-only with no specified non-color cue for the *selected node itself*. *Fix:* specify a selection ring/stroke change on the focused node plus the aria state.

## 3. Keyboard & focus

Verified good: roving tabindex ordered by minute, Enter opens popovers, Esc closes topmost, skip-link, focus moves to revealed heading on expansion, sorting keeps row focus, `Tab` follows reading order, no hover-only info, focus-visible everywhere (but see the light-pitch ring fail in §1).

- **[high]** Momentum scrub is pointer-only: "Hover/tap on the timeline scrubs a minute cursor" and mobile "scrub by tap-drag" (EXPERIENCE → Component Patterns → Momentum Timeline; Responsive table) — no arrow-key scrub is specified, so per-minute values are not keyboard-operable (2.1.1). Only goal markers are focusable. Worse, "tap-drag" contradicts the spec's own Interaction Primitives ban: "**Banned:** … drag interactions in v1." The data table at Expert altitude is a mitigation, not an excuse for an inoperable control on the flagship Tactical viz. *Fix:* make the scrubber a focusable slider (`role="slider"`, arrow keys = ±1 minute, aria-valuetext = minute + values) and resolve the drag contradiction (tap-to-position also works).
- **[medium]** Cluster z-order cycling is tap-only: "repeated taps cycle the stack's z-order" (EXPERIENCE → Pitch panel). The cluster's list popover presumably serves keyboard users, but neither keyboard cycling nor the popover's internal arrow-key navigation is specified. *Fix:* state that Enter on a cluster opens the list popover with focus on the first item and arrow-key navigation; z-cycling is a pointer redundancy, not the only path.
- **[medium]** Stat-tile glossary access: "Tapping a tile with a glossary term in its label opens the glossary tooltip" (EXPERIENCE → Stat tile) — but the tile is otherwise "static display," so is it focusable? A non-focusable tap target is a 2.1.1 fail. *Fix:* make the glossary *term* within the label the focusable trigger (consistent with the dotted-underline trigger spec), not the whole tile.
- **[low]** Pass-network "second tap clears" has no stated keyboard equivalent (Enter toggles? Esc clears?). *Fix:* Enter toggles isolation on the focused node; Esc clears.

## 4. Screen reader

Verified good: `role="figure"` + localized `aria-label` summaries; marker name/role/value strings; `aria-expanded` on shells; `aria-sort` + polite live region on sort; real `<table>` alternatives declared "the text alternative of record"; goal markers announce scorer + minute; `<html lang>` tracks locale.

- **[medium]** Language switch has no announcement: the toggle swaps *every string on the page* client-side and sets `<html lang>` (EXPERIENCE → Language toggle), but no live-region announcement or focus-management is specified for the swap — a screen-reader user gets a silently rewritten page (4.1.3 status messages). *Fix:* polite live-region announcement ("Idioma: Español" / "Language: English") on toggle.
- **[medium]** Heatmap table equivalence is unverifiable: "every viz has its data-table alternative" (EXPERIENCE → Visualization Layering), and Expert logs cover shots/crosses/passes/sets — but no tabular form is defined for the heatmap (what rows? zone grid with intensity values?). Without a defined schema, "genuinely equivalent" cannot be audited. *Fix:* specify the heatmap alternative (e.g., zone-by-zone table: pitch third × channel × intensity %) like the other logs.
- **[low]** Async bundle load: skeletons are specified (EXPERIENCE → State Patterns → Cold route load) but no `aria-busy`/loading announcement, so SR users may read a half-populated page with no status. *Fix:* `aria-busy` on loading regions + polite "Datos cargados" when the bundle lands.
- **[low]** Sticky headers/columns (Expert tables) can visually occlude the focused row on scroll — no scroll-margin/focus-into-view rule specified. *Fix:* note scroll-padding equal to sticky-header height.

## 5. Motion, touch, zoom

Verified good: `prefers-reduced-motion` fully specified (instant expand, no draw-in, no smooth-scroll, motion decorative-only); ≥44×44 hit areas including invisible d3 hit areas; vertical-only page scroll; wide tables scroll inside their own container with sticky player column (1.4.10 data-table exception applies); 200% zoom single-column Hero.

- **[medium]** 1.4.10 Reflow is specced at 390px/200% only — WCAG 2.1 AA requires no 2-D scrolling at **320 CSS px** (equivalent to 400% zoom at 1280px). The spines' floor is "390px … 200% zoom" (EXPERIENCE → Accessibility Floor; DESIGN → Layout). Pitch panels, paired stat tiles, and the comparison stack are unverified at 320px, and the Hero acceptance test would pass while the AA audit fails. *Fix:* extend the reflow requirement to 320px for all non-data-table content.
- **[medium]** Hit-area collision on dense shot maps: markers draw at ~8–14px but get ≥44px invisible hit areas (EXPERIENCE → Interaction Primitives); the cluster popover only covers *overlapping* markers (FR-24 forbids displacement). Two markers 20px apart — routine in a box-mouth cluster — have massively overlapping 44px targets with no specified disambiguation, so touch users get the wrong shot. *Fix:* define hit-area partition (Voronoi) and/or extend the cluster-list pattern to any markers whose hit areas collide, not only visual overlaps.
- **[low]** "Momentum … reduced height" at mobile plus tap-drag scrub concentrates a fine-grained 90+-minute slider into a short, narrow strip — motor-impaired users can't land on a minute. The slider fix in §3 (arrow keys, tap-to-position) resolves this; note it applies to touch too.

## 6. i18n accessibility

Verified good: Spanish-default pre-render matches default `<html lang>`; locale read before first paint; per-term policy table with tooltips for all terms; `Intl.NumberFormat` locale-correct numbers; "Ver los datos / View data" localized control names.

- **[medium]** Glossary tooltip vs 1.4.13: "Tap/hover/focus opens" (EXPERIENCE → Glossary tooltip) and Esc-closes-topmost is global, but **hoverable** (pointer can move onto the tooltip content — it contains a "Ver en el glosario" link the user must reach) and **persistent** (stays until dismissed/invalid) are not specified. A hover tooltip with an interactive link inside that vanishes on pointer-leave is a classic 1.4.13 + 2.1.1 fail. *Fix:* specify: remains open while pointer or focus is within trigger or panel; dismissible via Esc without moving focus; the link is reachable by Tab from the trigger.
- **[low]** English jargon inside Spanish UI ("xG", "sprint", "momentum") leans on the 3.1.2 "vernacular" exemption — defensible for these naturalized terms, and proper names are exempt, but section headings that embed retained-English terms (e.g. "Línea de momentum") will be pronounced by es-MX/es-ES TTS engines; the spec's blanket "per-element `lang` … not required" (EXPERIENCE → Accessibility Floor) is asserted, not tested. *Fix:* keep the assumption but add an acceptance note: spot-check the jargon terms with a Spanish screen-reader voice; add `lang="en"` spans only where pronunciation is unintelligible.
- **[low]** Glossary tooltip content locale on mixed pages: tooltip for "xG" shows "goles esperados" — fine — but the accessible name pattern for markers ("Tiro de {player}, minuto 63, xG 0,08, atajado") is specified in Spanish only; the EN variants of these aria strings are implied but not stated as locale-file entries. *Fix:* one line: all aria-label/aria-valuetext strings are locale-file keys under FR-30, never hardcoded.
