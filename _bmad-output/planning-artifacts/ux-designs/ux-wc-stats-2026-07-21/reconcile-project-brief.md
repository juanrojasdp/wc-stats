## 2. Contradictions

1. **Missing "incomplete" outcome in the shot/cross encoding — DESIGN vs. brief.** Brief Appendix B: legend maps five outcomes — "green=goal, light blue=on target, amber=off target, purple=blocked, **dark blue=incomplete**"; Appendix A-D includes cross locations/types. DESIGN → Data-visualization palette → Shot-outcome encoding lists only four outcomes (Goal / On target / Off target / Blocked) and even paraphrases the source hue families while silently omitting incomplete — yet declares the encoding applies to "shot maps **and cross maps**", where incomplete is a real outcome in the source data. Either a fifth token + marker shape is needed, or a logged decision that incomplete events are excluded/merged. **Not PRD-superseded as far as the spines record — needs parent attention.**
2. **Goalkeeping appended to the Tactical order.** Brief's MVP match-dashboard list ends at "set plays" (Domain E existing only in the extraction inventory); EXPERIENCE's Tactical Layer adds "→ goalkeeping" last. The spine explicitly attributes the order to PRD §4.6 / addendum §6 and flags the placement as an ASSUMPTION. **PRD-superseded / self-flagged — not a UX conflict.**
3. **Hero Layer internal tension touching the brief's header spec.** Brief puts lineups + formations in the match header (first item of the section order). EXPERIENCE's Hero contract says "nothing collapsible — it is simply *there*" while its own data-domain cell puts "lineups & formations in a compact disclosure". This is spine-internal inconsistency more than a brief conflict, but the brief is the reason lineups sit in the Hero at all, so the resolution (are lineups exempt from the no-collapse rule?) should be made explicit. **Minor — worth one clarifying sentence.**
4. **Naming drift, not conflict:** brief calls the timeline "possession / momentum timeline"; spines name it "Momentum Timeline" with possession carried separately as a Story Stat and in Key Statistics. No data is lost; recording here so the parent doesn't re-flag it.

No spine statement contradicts the brief's audience model, layer model, i18n axes, static constraint, or coordinate-fidelity rules.

## 3. Dropped qualitative ideas

- **Career-leverage / portfolio motivation** (Post-MVP Vision). The brief makes "portfolio piece combining data engineering, visualization, and football analytics" an explicit outcome. Neither spine acknowledges it; only DESIGN's "polish" differentiation line carries the flavor. As a decision tiebreaker ("when in doubt, choose the more demonstrable/impressive treatment") it has UX weight and is currently silent.
- **"Trapped in an unusable container" / liberation framing** (Problem Statement). The emotional pitch — extraordinary data locked in a hostile format — survives implicitly (UJ-2's "a number available nowhere else short of the 52-page PDF" is a good echo) but the mobile-hostility of the source ("unreadable on mobile") never surfaces as an explicit design rationale for the mobile-first stance.
- **"Free, open" identity** (Executive Summary). "Free" is structurally guaranteed; "open" (open project / passion-project ethos, tip-jar spirit) has no UX expression — e.g., nothing on `/about` about the project being open or a passion project, though the brief frames credits/methodology territory.
- **"Framed as a story"** — landed well (Story Stats, UJ-1 narrative arc); noted as *not* dropped.
- **Brief's example jargon terms** ("line breaks", "build up", "counter-press") — all three appear verbatim in the i18n table; noted as *not* dropped.
- **Domain D's quieter members** — "offering to receive" and "movement to receive" are distinctive PMSR content the brief inventories with evident enthusiasm (Appendix A-D); they carry differentiation value ("data available nowhere else") and are the only extraction-inventory viz candidates with zero presence in Visualization Layering. Overlaps with Coverage gap above; listed here because the loss is also one of ambition, not just traceability.

## 4. Verdict

Clean enough to finalize, with two items to fix first. The spines cover the brief's UX spine faithfully — audiences, layered disclosure, the 15-second goal, section order, i18n (resolved beyond what the brief asked), attribution posture, and the static/zero-cost constraint are all traceably present, and the brief's open i18n question is affirmatively closed. The two items that should not ship as-is: (1) the missing "incomplete" outcome in DESIGN's shot/cross encoding, which contradicts the source-data legend the brief documents and affects cross maps directly; (2) the dangling "search" reference in EXPERIENCE's IA, which cites a surface no pattern defines. Secondary, lower-stakes: decide the Domain D sub-viz question (offers/movement-to-receive/defensive actions) explicitly rather than by omission, and add one sentence resolving the Hero "nothing collapsible" vs. lineups-disclosure tension. The dropped qualitative items (portfolio motive, open-project ethos) are acceptable losses for spine documents but cheap to restore with a line each.
