# i18n & Terminology Review — wc-stats

Reviewed: `DESIGN.md` + `EXPERIENCE.md` (ux-wc-stats-2026-07-21). Policy under test: Spanish-first, es-CO/LatAm audience (Bogotá persona), English toggle.

## Overall verdict

**Fail the gate as-is; approvable after fixes.** The terminology table's core is genuinely good (bloque alto/medio/bajo, salida de balón, contrapresión, regate, balón parado, the xG and sprint jargon calls are all correct real-world usage). But there are two critical defects — one semantically inverted Spanish term on a core tactical surface, and an internally contradictory i18n render mechanic — plus a systemic coverage gap: the table covers tactical vocabulary but almost none of the labels the product will actually render most often (shot-outcome legends, goalkeeping, stage names, standings abbreviations, dates). Fixable in one pass; nothing structural.

## 1. The terminology table itself

- **[critical]** `forced turnover → pérdida forzada` inverts the metric's perspective (i18n table). FIFA-style "forced turnovers" are credited to the team that *forces* the loss — it is a defensive/pressing achievement. "Pérdidas forzadas" as a stat label on a team's pressing section or leaderboard reads as *that team's own giveaways* — the exact opposite meaning, on the pressing/Defensive Blocks surface Diego screenshots. Spanish analytics media renders this from the recovering side. *Fix:* "recuperaciones forzadas" (or "pérdidas forzadas al rival" where the losing-side framing is genuinely intended); never bare "pérdidas forzadas" for the forcing team's count.
- **[medium]** `high-speed run → carrera de alta velocidad` is a calque; Spanish physical-performance reporting says "carrera **a** alta velocidad" (i18n table). *Fix:* "carreras a alta velocidad" (plural in table/label contexts).
- **[medium]** `line height → altura de línea` reads like typography (CSS line-height), and nobody says it bare; broadcast/analytics usage is "altura de la línea (defensiva)" (i18n table; UJ-3 step 3 "Line Heights"). *Fix:* "altura de la línea defensiva", short label "altura de la línea".
- **[medium]** `step-in → irrupción` is a made-up label — nobody's usage — and the spec itself flags it unverified (i18n table [ASSUMPTION]). If the PMSR metric means a defender stepping out of the line to press, the established Spanish tactical term is "salto" (saltar a presionar); if it means carrying into the block, it's "conducción (al interior)". "Irrupción" matches neither community's vocabulary. *Fix:* resolve the PMSR definition before the content pass; candidate map: defender-steps-out → "salto"; ball-carry-entry → "conducción interior".
- **[medium]** `pressing` is used as a section concept ("pressing & Defensive Blocks", Progressive Disclosure Contract; UJ-2 step 2) but has **no row in the table** — and it is exactly the kind of term the Spanish-first policy must rule on ("presión"/"presión alta" is strong real usage; untranslated "pressing" is also naturalized). *Fix:* add a row; recommend translate → "presión" (section label "Presión y bloques defensivos"), consistent with the already-chosen "contrapresión".
- **[low]** `line break → ruptura de línea`: real usage is plural — "ruptura/rotura de líneas", "pases que rompen líneas"; FIFA's own Spanish EFI materials use the plural (i18n table). *Fix:* "rupturas de líneas" in labels.
- **[low]** `team length → longitud del equipo` is understandable but slightly lab-speak; broadcast Spanish says a team is "largo/corto". *Fix:* acceptable as-is; "largo del equipo" is the more colloquial alternative — pick one and note it.
- **[low]** `phases of play → fases de juego`: the more common form is "fases **del** juego". *Fix:* "fases del juego".
- **[low]** `set play → balón parado` is correct and universal, but the target market note is worth logging: Colombian broadcast overwhelmingly says "pelota quieta". Keeping the neutral "balón parado" is defensible under "no regionalisms" — log the decision so it isn't relitigated.

Rows explicitly verified as correct real usage (no finding): contrapresión, salida de balón, bloque alto/medio/bajo, red de pases, zonas de velocidad, regate, segunda jugada, progresión de balón, recepción en el último tercio, xG-as-jargon, sprint-as-jargon, momentum-as-tooltip (with "Línea de momentum" as product name).

## 2. Coverage

- **[high]** Shot-outcome legend labels are absent from the table, yet they render on the product's signature surface (DESIGN → shot-outcome encoding: Goal / On target / Off target / Blocked, English only). These need ruled-on Spanish with a LatAm decision: "on target" is "al arco" in LatAm broadcast ("a puerta" is peninsular). *Fix:* add rows — gol / al arco / desviado / bloqueado — and mirror them in the legend spec and the "Ver los datos" table headers.
- **[high]** The entire goalkeeping domain (Tactical section `#goalkeeping`, Domain E, "Involvement + distribution summary") has zero terminology coverage — including the single most audience-sensitive word on the site: "portero" vs **"arquero"** (the LatAm/Colombian term). Also missing: atajada (save — note the spec's own aria example already says "atajado"), salidas, distribución, mano a mano. *Fix:* add a goalkeeping block to the table; recommend "arquero" and "atajadas" for the LatAm register, section label "Arqueros" or "Portería" (decide once).
- **[high]** No date/month rendering rule anywhere in either spine. Number formatting is specified (Voice and Tone), dates are not — yet the Hub renders 104 match dates. es-CO dates are "21 de julio de 2026", lowercase months, and `Intl.DateTimeFormat('es-CO')` should be mandated exactly as `Intl.NumberFormat` is. *Fix:* add a date-format rule beside the number rule, with one worked example per locale (and a decision on kickoff-time/timezone display across the three host countries).
- **[medium]** Stage names: UJ-1 shows "Octavos de final" but the full 2026 set is uncovered — critically including the new round of 32, whose Spanish is the awkward-but-real "dieciseisavos de final" (Spanish media already uses it). Also: fase de grupos, cuartos de final, semifinal, tercer puesto, final. *Fix:* enumerate all stage strings in the locale files/table.
- **[medium]** Standings/leaderboard scaffolding absent: column abbreviations (PJ, G, E, P, GF, GC, DG, Pts — DESIGN covers only the G/E/P result chips), position names (arquero/defensa/mediocampista/delantero), lineup labels (titulares/suplentes, alineaciones, formación). *Fix:* add a "table & roster labels" block.
- **[medium]** Key Statistics block terms unruled: corner ("tiro de esquina"), offside — a genuine LatAm decision ("posición adelantada"/"fuera de lugar" vs peninsular "fuera de juego") — faltas, duelos (UJ-2 says "duels"), and "cross/cross map" → "centros / mapa de centros". *Fix:* add rows; recommend "posición adelantada" for the target register.

## 3. Consistency

- **[medium]** The aria example "Tiro de {player}, minuto 63, xG 0,08, **atajado**" (EXPERIENCE → Accessibility Floor) uses an outcome that does not exist in the DESIGN shot taxonomy (Goal/On target/Off target/Blocked — a saved shot is "on target"). The spec's own copy invents a fifth category the legend can't show. *Fix:* align the aria vocabulary to the four ruled outcomes ("al arco") or add "saved" to the taxonomy deliberately.
- **[medium]** "Clasificaciones" as the leaderboards label (UJ-4 step 2) collides with standings: in LatAm Spanish "la clasificación" *is* the league table, and the Hub carries both standings (FR-25) and leaderboards (FR-26) — one word pointing at two different surfaces. *Fix:* standings → "Tabla de posiciones", leaderboards → "Líderes del torneo" (or "Líderes").
- **[medium]** Empty-state copy "El informe oficial no incluye esta página." (State Patterns) — "página" means the PDF page, but in a web UI it reads as *this web page doesn't exist*, which is alarming next to working content. *Fix:* "El informe oficial no incluye esta sección."
- **[low]** Canonical control string drift: Voice and Tone's Do column says "Ver los datos de este gráfico" while Component Patterns specifies the control as "Ver los datos / View data". Two canonical forms of the same string invite ad-hoc copies. *Fix:* pick one (the short control string) and make the Voice example match.
- **[low]** Flow narratives use English terms the table has ruled Spanish ("Line Heights, Defensive Block distribution, formation usage" UJ-3; "pressing & Defensive Blocks" UJ-2 and the layer table). Spec narrative isn't UI copy, but the spine is the document implementers copy labels from. *Fix:* one sentence noting section labels come from the i18n table, and/or use the es labels in the layer-order list.
- **[low]** Expert column-group tabs "En posesión / Sin posesión / Físico" (Responsive table): fine, but broadcast Spanish more naturally says "Con balón / Sin balón". *Fix:* optional swap; if kept, add the pair to the terminology table so it's ruled, not ad-hoc.

## 4. Mechanics

- **[critical]** The no-flash promise contradicts the render model. Language toggle spec says locale is "read before first paint to avoid flash" (Component Patterns) — but every route is pre-rendered **Spanish HTML** (IA). For a persisted-EN user, the Spanish DOM exists before any script runs: either the user sees a Spanish flash (FOUC) before the client-side swap, or a blocking inline script rewrites strings pre-paint — and either way React hydration will mismatch (server text ≠ client text), which in Next static export means hydration errors or a deliberate two-pass render. The spec asserts the outcome without owning the mechanism. *Fix:* specify the mechanism: two-pass render with `suppressHydrationWarning` on text nodes is not viable at this scale; realistic options are (a) accept one-frame Spanish flash for EN users and say so, or (b) inline script sets `<html lang>` + a locale class pre-paint and all strings render client-side post-hydration with the skeleton covering the gap. Log the choice.
- **[high]** Single-tree i18n consequences are unowned (IA [ASSUMPTION]): English content is invisible to search engines (no `/en/` URLs, no hreflang), and every link Diego shares *after switching to EN* produces a **Spanish** link preview (`og:title`/description are the pre-rendered Spanish). The spec notes "Spanish-default content for SEO/link previews" as if it were only an implementation detail. *Fix:* explicitly accept and log the tradeoffs (no EN SEO, es-only share cards), and confirm no `hreflang` is emitted (a lone self-referencing one is worse than none).
- **[high]** Text expansion is unaddressed at the tightest layouts: Spanish runs ~20–30% longer, and the system's labels are 11px ALL-CAPS with 0.08em tracking in 12px-gap tiles at 390px (`stat-label`, `label-caps`). "VELOCIDAD MÁXIMA" vs "TOP SPEED"; "CARRERAS A ALTA VELOCIDAD" as a table column head will not fit. No wrap/truncate/abbreviation rule exists in either spine. *Fix:* add a label-overflow rule (two-line wrap allowed in tiles; ruled abbreviations for column heads, e.g. "VEL. MÁX.", with the full term in the header tooltip/aria-label).
- **[medium]** Sorting: client-side sort "on any column head" (Component Patterns) includes name columns, but nothing mandates `Intl.Collator('es')` — default string compare missorts Á/É/Ñ (Ñíguez, Álvarez sort after Z). *Fix:* mandate `Intl.Collator('es', {sensitivity:'base'})` for all text sorts, same normative style as the `Intl.NumberFormat` rule.
- **[medium]** `localStorage` unavailability (Safari private mode, blocked storage) has no specified fallback for `wcstats.locale`/`wcstats.theme`. *Fix:* try/catch wrapper; fall back to in-memory es default; toggle still works for the session.
- **[low]** Number formatting is otherwise consistent everywhere checked (`xG 1,24`, `10,4 km`, `36,8 km/h` in UJ-4, aria "xG 0,08" — all es-CO comma). One nit: RAE prescribes a space before % ("62 %"); "62%" is fine for UI but log it as a deliberate choice so locale QA doesn't churn on it.

## 5. Glossary flow

- **[medium]** Diego's bridge is missing: terms ruled "translate" surface *only* the Spanish term in the ES UI, and UJ-2 has Diego toggling the whole site to EN just to recover blog-standard terminology. A bilingual reader (most of tactical Twitter-es) can't map "salida de balón" ↔ "build-up" without flipping the entire interface. This isn't a policy contradiction — Spanish-first stands — but the glossary should carry the mapping. *Fix:* every glossary tooltip and `/glossary` entry shows the counterpart term as a subtitle ("salida de balón — en: build-up"); zero cost to Mariana, removes Diego's toggle dance for citation.
- **[low]** Mariana's side is well served (Hero bans un-tooltipped tactical vocabulary; xG tooltip "goles esperados" is right). One gap: the Hero contract says tooltips gate tactical vocabulary, but the Story Stats tile labels themselves (posesión, xG, distancia) are the terms — confirm the "tap tile label opens tooltip" affordance (Component Patterns) is discoverable, e.g. the dotted-underline trigger style applies inside tile labels too. *Fix:* one sentence in the stat-tile pattern confirming the trigger styling applies.
